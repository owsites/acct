Frequently asked questions

What are the benefits of ACCT Membership?

  • Be Recognized as a Certified Professional: By joining ACCT, you’ll be part of a national registry that validates your credentials, making it easier for employers, embassies, and global organizations to recognize your expertise.
  • Access Global Opportunities: ACCT is actively working to establish connections with international organizations like SCCM (UK) and healthcare authorities in the Middle East, opening doors for Indian CCTs to work abroad without extra hurdles.
  • Advance Your Career with Education and Research: As a member, you’ll have access to free CME activities and the opportunity to publish in India's first-ever Critical Care Technology Journal, positioning you as a leader in your field.
  • Shape the Future of CCT in India: Be a part of the movement that advocates for mandatory CCT inclusion in every ICU across India, influencing policy and strengthening the recognition of our profession at the national level.

Why is there a Fee? Why make payment?

The membership fee supports the ongoing efforts of the Association of Critical Care Technologists (ACCT) to advance the CCT profession in India. This fee allows us to:

  • Advocate for Policy Changes: ACCT is actively working with healthcare policymakers to recognize Critical Care Technologists at a national level, pushing for initiatives like mandatory CCTs in every ICU.
  • Provide Educational Resources: Your fee helps fund free CME activities, workshops, and educational programs to keep you updated on the latest in critical care technology.
  • Create Networking and Career Opportunities: ACCT uses membership fees to establish partnerships with international organizations like ISCCM and SCCM (UK), opening doors for Indian CCTs to work abroad and creating a strong professional network.
  • Develop Professional Tools and Resources: The fee contributes to creating valuable tools such as the National CCT Registry, which validates your credentials for employers and international agencies, and our upcoming Critical Care Technology Journal.
Your membership fee is an investment in your career and in the future of the CCT profession in India.

What’s the difference between registration with ACCT vs AAHP/AHCPI?

ACCT is exclusively focused on Critical Care Technologists, while AAHP/ahcpi covers all allied healthcare professions. Unlike general organizations, ACCT is actively fighting to include CCTs as a named entity in the NCAHP Act and working to establish mandatory CCTs in every ICU. ACCT provides tailored advocacy, education, and career opportunities specifically for CCTs, ensuring your profession gets the recognition and growth it deserves.

Will I get an ACCT ID Card and Registration Certificate?

Yes. Once your membership is verified after documents processing and fee validation, you will recieve via post your ACCT ID Card (QR Code Embedded) and Certification of Registration for the National Register of CCTs. Note the all student members receive a digital ID card.
